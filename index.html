Athens 2004

The 2004 Summer Olympics, officially known as the Games of the XXVIII Olympiad, were an international multi-sport event held in Athens, Greece, from 13 to 29 August 2004. The games were the first to be held in the 21st century, and the first to be held in Greece since the 1896 Summer Olympics in Athens. Athens 2004 was the first Olympics to be held in a city that had previously hosted the event, and the first to be held in a city that had not hosted the event since the 1904 Summer Olympics in St. Louis, Missouri, United States.

Beijing 2008

The 2008 Summer Olympics, officially the Games of the XXIX Olympiad and also known as Beijing 2008, were an international multisport event held from 8 to 24 August 2008, in Beijing, China.

Vancouver 2010

The 2010 Winter Olympics, officially known as the XXI Olympic Winter Games and also known as Vancouver 2010, were an international winter multi-sport event held from February 12 to 28, 2010 in Vancouver, British Columbia, Canada, with some events held in the surrounding suburbs of Richmond, West Vancouver and the University of British Columbia, and in the nearby resort town of Whistler.

London 2012

The 2012 Summer Olympics, officially known as the Games of the XXX Olympiad and commonly known as London 2012, was an international multi-sport event that was held from 27 July to 12 August 2012 in London, United Kingdom.

Rio de Janeiro 2016

The 2016 Summer Olympics, officially the Games of the XXXI Olympiad and also known as Rio 2016, was an international multi-sport event held from 5 to 21 August 2016 in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil.

Tokyo 2020

The 2020 Summer Olympics, officially the Games of the XXXII Olympiad and also known as Tokyo 2020, was an international multi-sport event held from 23 July to 8 August 2021 in Tokyo, Japan.

Olympic Games History

The Olympic Games, which originated in ancient Greece as many as 3,000 years ago, were revived in the late 19th century and have become the world's preeminent sporting competition. From the 8th century B.C. to the 4th century A.D., the Games were held every four years in Olympia, located in the western Peloponnese peninsula, in honor of the god Zeus. The first modern Olympics took place in 1896 in Athens, and featured 280 participants from 12 nations, competing in 43 events. Since 1994, the Summer and Winter Olympic Games have been held separately and have alternated every two years. The 2022 Winter Olympics will be held from February 4 to February 20, 2022 in Beijing, China and feature figure skating, ice hockey, curling and more.

Olympic games added per date

The Olympic games of 1896 showcased athletics, cycling, fencing, gymnastics, shooting, swimming, tennis, weight lifting, and wrestling, according to the International Olympic Committee (IOC).

In 1900, football, rugby, golf, and rowing were added to the lineup along with archery water polo, and sailing, according to the IOC. This was also the first year that women could compete in the games, the IOC's website says.

Four years later, in 1904, the IOC added boxing and diving to the roster while golf was cut.

Field hockey, figure skating, and motorboating were added in 1908, the IOC website says.

While women could compete in 1900, they couldn't swim in the games until 1912, when the modern pentathlon and short racewalking were also introduced, according to the IOC. Equestrian events also had their official debut, according to the same source.

The first Winter Olympics took place in 1924, and the IOC added various skiing competitions, ice hockey, curling, and bobsleigh to the games. Rugby was cut after this year, according to the IOC.

By 1928, skeleton and women's gymnastics were a part of the Olympics, according to the IOC. Skeleton was dropped after this year, picked back up in 1948, and then dropped again immediately after, according to the same source.

In 1932, long-distance walking became an Olympic sport with the first racewalk competition over 30 miles long, according to the IOC.

In 1936, basketball, field handball, women's football, canoeing became a part of the competition, as well as Alpine skiing, according to the IOC.

In 1960, the IOC added biathlon and women's speed skating to the games.

In 1964, the IOC brought volleyball, luge, and men's judo to the world-renowned event.

In 1972, the IOC debuted men's indoor handball.

Women's basketball, handball, and rowing were added in 1976, according to the IOC.

In 1984, the games added women's cycling road, shooting, artistic swimming, and rhythmic gymnastics, according to the IOC.

Table tennis became an Olympic sport in 1988 while tennis came back after a 64-year hiatus, the IOC's website says. Women's cycling track also made its debut, according to the IOC.

Baseball, badminton, roller speed skating, short track speed skating, and women's judo became Olympic sports in 1992, according to the IOC.

The IOC added mountain biking to the games in 1996.

Snowboarding and women's ice hockey debuted in 1998, according to the IOC.

Taekwondo made its Olympic debut in 2000 along with the trampoline gymnastics, triathlon, women's pentathlon, water polo, race walking, and weightlifting, according to the IOC.

In 2002, the IOC brought back skeleton for the first time in 50 years.

Women's wrestling became an Olympic sport in 2004, according to the IOC.

BMX racing and the 10-kilometer swimming marathon were added to the games in 2008, according to the IOC.

The IOC added freestyle skiing, women's boxing, and mixed tennis doubles to the 2012 Olympic Games.

In 2016, men's rugby and golf came back to the Olympics for the first time in about a century while women competed in rugby and sailing for the first time ever.

In 2020, karate, skateboarding, sport climbing, BMX freestyle, and surfing were added to the Olympic games, according to the IOC. The event was rescheduled for summer 2021.
